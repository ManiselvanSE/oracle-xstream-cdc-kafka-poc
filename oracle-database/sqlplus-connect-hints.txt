sqlplus — passwords with # or other shell-special characters
================================================================

In bash/zsh, an unquoted # starts a comment. If your SYS password contains #,
this WRONG form truncates at #:

  sqlplus sys/ConFL#_uent12@//host:1521/service AS SYSDBA
                 ^-- everything after # is ignored; sqlplus may get wrong password.

Fix: put the entire user/password@connect_identifier in SINGLE QUOTES:

  sqlplus 'sys/YOUR_PASSWORD@//10.0.0.29:1521/your.service.name' AS SYSDBA

Or use DOUBLE QUOTES around the whole connect string (password inside):

  sqlplus "sys/YOUR_PASSWORD@//10.0.0.29:1521/your.service.name" AS SYSDBA

Run a script after connecting:

  sqlplus 'sys/YOUR_PASSWORD@//host:1521/service' AS SYSDBA @verify-mtx-cdc-prereqs.sql

Avoid pasting real passwords into shell history: use /nolog then CONNECT, or a wallet.

Example /nolog:

  sqlplus /nolog
  SQL> CONNECT sys@//10.0.0.29:1521/your.service.name AS SYSDBA
  Enter password: (hidden)
